Pad Thai - Classic Thai Stir-Fried Noodles

Description

Pad Thai is Thailand's most famous street-food dish: rice noodles stir-fried with a sweet-sour-savory sauce made from tamarind, fish sauce, and palm sugar, tossed with egg, tofu or shrimp, and finished with crunchy peanuts and fresh lime. The balance of flavors is key - no single taste should dominate.

Ingredients

2-3 servings

Noodles and proteins

  • 200g flat rice noodles
  • 150g shrimp, peeled & deveined (or chicken, tofu, or a mix)
  • 100g firm tofu, diced
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil

Pad Thai Sauce

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1½ tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce (optional, for depth)
  • ½-1 tsp chili flakes (optional)

Vegetables & Garnish

  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 spring onions, cut into 5 cm pieces
  • 3 tbsp roasted peanuts, crushed
  • lime wedges
  • Optional: extra chili flakes

Steps

  1. Place rice noodles in warm water for 20-30 minutes until pliable but not soft. Drain well.
  2. In a bowl, mix tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, soy sauce, and chili flakes. Stir until the sugar dissolves. Taste: it should be tangy, slightly sweet, and savory.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. Add shrimp and cook until just pink. Remove and set aside.
  4. Add garlic and tofu to the pan. Stir-fry until fragrant and lightly golden.
  5. Push everything to one side, crack in the eggs, scramble briefly, then mix into the tofu.
  6. Add drained noodles and pour in the sauce. Toss quickly, letting the noodles absorb the liquid. Add a splash of water if needed.
  7. Return shrimp to the pan. Add bean sprouts and spring onions. Toss for 30-60 seconds - vegetables should stay crisp.
  8. Plate immediately and top with crushed peanuts, chili flakes, and lime wedges.

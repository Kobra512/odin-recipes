Käsespätzle

Description

Spätzle is a type of pasta dish famous in southern Germany and Austria. They are way more fluffy than ordinary italian pasta and are almost always made completely fresh. There are different methods for forming the raw dough into the typical shape, which is either

  • little droplets of dough
  • longer noodles of dough, similar to Udon noodles
For the former you'll need a special kind of kitchen utensil called a “Spätzlehobel” which might be really difficult to get your hands on, depending on where you live.

The latter is achieved by putting the flat dough on a board over the boiling water and getting pieces of the dough inside the pot with a kind of cutting-scraping movement. You can get a general idea by watching this short video

Ingredients

4 servings

  • 400g of flour
  • 4 eggs
  • 125ml of water
  • salt
  • 4l of salted boiling water
  • 200g of grated Emmentaler (or any other similar cheese)
  • 250g of onions
  • 60g of butter

Steps

  1. Mix flour, eggs, water, salt to a thick dough. Mix until you can see some bubbles
  2. Let the dough rest for 10 to 30 minutes
  3. Heat the salted water until it's boiling
  4. Take some of the dough an cook it in the salted water using the method shown in the video
  5. When all the dough swims on the surface the it out with a slotted spoon and put them inside a casserolle
  6. Repeat the last two steps until you have cooked all the dough
  7. Mix the cheese with the cooked Spätzle and let the cheese melt by the heat of the spätzle, maybe put them somewhere warm, e.g. microvawe or oven
  8. Cut the onion into thick rings and fry them inside a pan with the butter until they have a golden-brown color
  9. Put the onions over the Spätzle and serve
